Starting your freelance activity in Luxembourg? Invoicing is a crucial aspect of your business. This guide explains how to create compliant invoices and manage your tax obligations.

Freelancer Status in Luxembourg

In Luxembourg, a freelancer (or self-employed person) typically operates under one of these statuses:

  • Sole proprietorship: the most common status for starting out
  • Single-person LLC (SARL-S): a simplified limited liability company
  • Liberal profession: for certain regulated activities

Regardless of your status, you must follow the same invoicing rules.

VAT Registration

Before starting to invoice, you must obtain an intra-community VAT number from the Administration de l'Enregistrement et des Domaines (AED).

Registration Procedure

  1. Obtain an establishment authorization from the Ministry of Economy
  2. Register with the Commercial Register (RCS) if applicable
  3. Request VAT registration via MyGuichet.lu
  4. Receive your number in LU + 8 digits format

Mandatory Information on Your Invoices

As a freelancer, your invoices must contain:

Your Information

  • Full name or company name
  • Business address in Luxembourg
  • VAT number (LU12345678)
  • Establishment authorization number
  • Your RCS number if applicable

Client Information

  • Name or company name
  • Complete address
  • VAT number (mandatory for business clients)

Service Details

  • Unique invoice number and sequential
  • Issue date
  • Detailed description of services rendered
  • Number of hours or days (recommended)
  • Net unit rate
  • Net, VAT, and gross amounts
  • Applicable VAT rate

Which VAT Rate to Apply?

As a freelancer, you generally apply the standard rate of 17% for most service provisions.

Special Cases

Situation Applicable VAT
Business client in Luxembourg VAT 17%
Business client in the EU Exempt (reverse charge)
Client outside EU Exempt
Individual in Luxembourg VAT 17%

Invoicing Foreign Clients

Business Client in the EU

If your client is a business in another EU country:

  1. Verify their VAT number on the VIES system
  2. Do not apply VAT on your invoice
  3. Add the mention: "VAT exemption - Article 44 of the law of 12 February 1979"
  4. Indicate the VAT number of the client on the invoice

Client Outside the EU

For clients located outside the European Union, the service is exempt from VAT. Mention "VAT exemption - Service outside EU".

Invoice Numbering

Your invoices must follow chronological and continuous numbering:

  • No gaps in the sequence
  • Free but consistent format (e.g.: 2026-001, 2026-002...)
  • One series per year

Tips for Beginning Freelancers

  1. Use appropriate software from the start to avoid errors
  2. Keep all invoices (issued and received) for 10 years
  3. Separate your accounts personal and professional
  4. Invoice quickly (within 15 days of the service)
  5. Verify VAT numbers of your EU clients before invoicing
  6. Plan your declarations ahead to avoid penalties
  7. Consult an accountant for complex questions
